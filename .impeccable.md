## Design Context

### Users

The primary user is the project owner, monitoring World Cup predictions, live
match state, and market movement for extended periods on a large desktop
display. The core job is rapid situational awareness: identify the current
match, material probability or market changes, model performance, and the next
fixtures without navigating away from the main dashboard.

### Brand Personality

Compact, efficient, professional, restrained, and strongly real-time. The
interface should feel like a dependable operations console rather than a
marketing dashboard: calm under normal conditions, immediately legible when
something changes, and dense without becoming noisy.

### Aesthetic Direction

Retain the existing dark control-room visual language: tinted near-black
surfaces, hairline structural borders, compact tabular data, and semantic
emerald/amber/rose accents. Avoid gradients, ornamental effects, oversized
hero metrics, excessive card nesting, and whitespace that does not improve
scanning. Desktop large-screen monitoring is the primary design target; tablet
and mobile remain functional adaptations.

### Design Principles

1. Prioritize the live match and actionable market state; supporting research
   and historical metrics remain secondary.
2. Increase information density through alignment, grouping, and compact
   spacing—not by shrinking text below comfortable monitoring size.
3. Use a consistent 4/8/12/16px spacing rhythm, with tight spacing inside a
   data group and clearer separation between groups.
4. Preserve the three-column, single-screen desktop command-center layout;
   eliminate dead space by top-aligning content and using deliberate panel
   sections rather than vertical distribution tricks.
5. Communicate state with labels and values as well as color, maintain strong
   contrast, and respect reduced-motion preferences.
